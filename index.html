Mumbai Floods July 2005

Facts of the Time

  1. Large numbers of people were stranded on the road, lost their homes, and many walked for long distances back home from work that evening. The floods were caused by the eighth heaviest ever recorded 24-hour rainfall figure of 994 mm.
  2. Severely affected areas were Raigad, Chiplun, Khed, Ratnagiri and Kalyan in Maharashtra and the southern state of Goa
  3. All the ground floor flats were under water
  4. Western and Central Railways did not run their local services for a number of days
  5. In agriculture sector, approximately 5.5 lakh hectares of land had suffered crop losses
  6. More than 20,000 classrooms had been damaged, and 97 school buildings had collapsed. About 437 Primary Health Centers, rural hospitals, and residential premises for health personnel had been damaged by flooding. The Public Works Department estimated that it would require Rs. 1,200 crores for repairing roads and bridges damaged by flooding
  7. About 8 lakh families are badly affected by floods, and the total amount that may have to be disbursed may go up to Rs. 400 crores
  8. 52 local trains damaged & 37,000 autorickshaws spoilt & 4,000 taxis & 900 BEST buses damaged & 10,000 trucks and tempos grounded
